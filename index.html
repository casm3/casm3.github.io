Dr. Carlos Melo

Researcher, Data Analyst, Professor, Programmer and not so Pro gamer

About Me.

I hold a Ph.D. (2017 - 2021) and a Master's degree (2014 - 2016) in Computer Science from the Center of Informatics at the Federal University of Pernambuco (CIn-UFPE). I also have a specialization in Cloud Computing (2021) from the Polytechnic School of Pernambuco (POLI-UPE) and a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science (2009 - 2013) from the Federal Rural University of Pernambuco (UFRPE).

Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Computer Science Graduate Program at the Federal University of Piauí (PPGCC-UFPI), focusing on research related to performance and dependability modeling and evaluation of Blockchain-based platforms.

Previously, I worked as a web development instructor (2021 - 2024) at the Trybe Programming School (Trybe), where I was responsible for the Computer Science module with Python and the elective Backend development with Java. My role included designing syllabi, developing automated projects, and producing both written and video content.

I served as a Section Leader (2021) in the Code in Place initiative by Stanford University, and as an Information Security instructor (2021) at Uni São Miguel, and taught Computer Networks and General Systems Theory (2018 - 2019) at Faculdade Alpha.

Additionally, I was a research assistant in a project between the Center of Informatics at UFPE and Motorola (2015 - 2017), working on applications for data synchronization services between mobile devices and cloud platforms.
